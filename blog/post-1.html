Getting Started with Heavy Haul Route Surveys

John Doe Senior Route Surveyor
• January 15, 2024 • 5 min read

Route surveying is a critical component of heavy haul transportation. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the essential steps and best practices for conducting effective route surveys.

Understanding the Basics of Route Surveying

Route surveying for heavy haul transportation involves carefully examining and documenting the entire transport route to ensure safe and efficient delivery of oversized loads. Key aspects include:

  • Identifying potential obstacles and hazards
  • Measuring bridge and overhead clearances
  • Assessing road conditions and weight restrictions
  • Documenting turn radiuses and sweep paths
  • Planning for temporary infrastructure modifications if needed

Essential Equipment and Tools

To conduct a thorough route survey, you'll need the following equipment:

  • GPS devices for accurate positioning and mapping
  • Laser measuring tools for precise clearance measurements
  • High-resolution cameras for documentation
  • Mobile devices with route survey software
  • Safety equipment (high-visibility vests, cones, etc.)

"The success of a heavy haul transport operation heavily depends on the accuracy and thoroughness of the initial route survey."

Step-by-Step Survey Process

1. Pre-Survey Planning

Before heading out to the field:

  • Review load dimensions and weight
  • Study satellite imagery and maps
  • Identify potential problem areas
  • Check weather conditions
  • Obtain necessary permits and authorizations

2. Field Survey Execution

During the actual survey:

  • Document all measurements systematically
  • Take photographs from multiple angles
  • Note road conditions and potential hazards
  • Identify safe parking and turning areas
  • Mark GPS coordinates of critical points

3. Post-Survey Analysis

After completing the field survey:

  • Compile all data and measurements
  • Create detailed route maps
  • Identify required permits and escorts
  • Develop contingency plans
  • Prepare comprehensive survey report

Common Challenges and Solutions

Be prepared to address these common challenges:

  • Low bridges and overhead obstacles
  • Sharp turns and narrow roads
  • Weight-restricted bridges
  • Urban areas with multiple utilities
  • Construction zones and temporary obstacles

Best Practices for Success

Follow these best practices to ensure survey accuracy:

  • Always double-check critical measurements
  • Document everything thoroughly with photos and notes
  • Consider seasonal changes and weather impacts
  • Communicate with local authorities and utility companies
  • Use digital tools for better accuracy and efficiency

Conclusion

A thorough route survey is the foundation of any successful heavy haul transport operation. By following these guidelines and best practices, you'll be well-equipped to conduct professional route surveys that ensure safe and efficient transport operations.
